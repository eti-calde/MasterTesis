## TL;DR (claim under re-verification)

For the **inverse** bathymetry problem, the **primitive** SWE residual
form is more robust than primitive-conservative or conservative
(reversing Tian et al. 2025's forward-problem ranking). This was the
finding under the legacy pipeline and is what justified flipping the
production default to `primitive` (batch #7). The azirafel re-run
either confirms or refines the magnitude.

## Setup

- Same ground truth, observations, training budget for all three forms.

  residual form).
- 3 seeds per form (9 runs total).

## Why it matters

To the best of our knowledge no prior work has compared SWE residual
forms specifically for **PINN-based bathymetry inversion**. Tian et
al. (2025) recommend primitive-conservative for forward solvers
(better conditioning of the residual), but in the inverse setting the
$A$-matrix weighting deforms the loss landscape — adding local minima
that catch L-BFGS. Primitive sacrifices marginally less peak accuracy
but converges reliably.
