# Experiment 5 — 3D Thacker Paraboloid Report

**Date**: 2026-05-01
**Case**: SWASHES §4.2.2 axisymmetric Thacker paraboloid (Thacker 1981)
**Status**: Converged after a 4-round diagnostic loop

---

## Summary

Closed basin $z_b(r) = -h_0(1 - r^2/a^2)$ with $h_0 = 0.1$ m, $a = 1$ m on a $4 \times 4$ m domain, 3 oscillation periods (~6.7 s). The paraboloid changes sign: −0.1 m at the center, +0.7 m at the corners. **75.5% of the domain is always dry** (the rim rises above mean water level). No external forcing — dynamics are driven purely by the IC.

**Headline finding**: η-only or η+u+v observations alone are **insufficient** to break the $(h, z_b)$ equifinality in this closed basin. Even with 30 time snapshots and a heavily weighted IC, the inverse PINN locks into a "shallow basin" branch where $\eta = h + z_b$ is fit but $h$ is half its true magnitude and $z_b$ is uniformly offset by ~70 mm. This contrasts with Exp 4 (2D tidal), where boundary forcing provides an external anchor that does break the equifinality.

**Resolution**: Adding a direct $z_b$ supervision at IC — using the already-known $h_{IC}$ and observed $\eta(t=0)$ to compute $z_b = \eta_{obs}(t=0) - h_{IC}$ at every IC point — anchors the bath net inside the wet disk and brings recovery to **9.84 mm RMSE** (matching Exp 2's 5 mm and Exp 4's 6 mm in the same dimensional ballpark).

### v2 baseline (`pinn_inverse.py` `__main__`)
- η-only observations, default weights
- Final: **zb_rmse_wet = 56 mm at LBFGS 840** (plateau)
- Failure mode: predicted $z_b$ flat at ~+0.05 m across wet disk; uniform red error ~+0.07 m

### Round 1 (`improve_baseline.py`)
Code-level fixes from `Notes/04-Lessons/L5-Friccion-BCs-Formas.md` and `SWE-Equation-Forms.md`:
- **Primitive-conservative residual** (Tian 2025): `R = A·r` with $A = [[1,0,0],[u,h,0],[v,0,h]]$ in `swe_residual_2d`. Auto-balances momentum vs continuity by depth.
- **Wet-biased collocation**: 80% of 12000 collocation points in ever-wet cells (was: 25% wet by random uniform). 9600 wet + 2400 dry.
- **`lambda_dry_zb` raised 100 → 1000**: rim anchor.
- Added Stage A: pretrain bath net on dry-rim regression for 3000 epochs (assumed Fourier features would extrapolate paraboloid into wet disk).
- **Result: 49.7 mm**. Modest 11% improvement. Stage A failed: rim_mse → 1e-7 but `zb_rmse_wet` stayed 200 mm — Fourier-feature MLPs do not extrapolate $r^2$ behavior beyond the training annulus.

### Round 2 (`improve_baseline_v2.py`)
- Add **η + u + v observations** (was η-only). Per Exp 1 results, velocity provides 4× improvement.
- **Result: 50.5 mm**. **Velocity did not help**. Loss components show data fit worsened slightly; the equifinality is structural, not observational.

### Round 3 (`improve_baseline_v3.py`)
- **`lambda_ic` raised 100 → 2000**: 20× heavier IC penalty to force $h$ to match its known initial profile.
- **Result: 49.8 mm**. **Heavy IC weight did not help either.** IC loss does drop (raw IC RMSE 28 mm → 4 mm at sampled points), but $z_b$ stays the same. Diagnosis: IC supervision constrains the *solution* net's $h$, not the *bath* net directly. The bath net only sees the data residual `η_pred − η_obs`, which the optimizer is happy to minimize via the wrong (h, z_b) branch.

### Round 4 (`improve_baseline_v4.py`) — succeeds
**Insight**: at IC, both $h_{IC}$ (analytical / measured initial state) and $\eta_{obs}(t=0)$ (observed) are known. Therefore $z_b = \eta_{obs}(t=0) - h_{IC}$ is a *direct, dense supervision* of the bath net at every IC point. This generalizes the rim's `dry_zb` mechanism (which works only where $h \equiv 0$) into the wet disk.

Implementation: in `pinn_inverse.py`, store `zb_target_ic = eta_obs[0] - h_ic` at IC sampling time. Add a new loss term:
$$\mathcal{L}_{zb,IC} = \lambda_{zb,IC} \cdot \frac{1}{N_{IC}} \sum_i (\hat z_b(x_i, y_i) - (\eta_{obs}(0, x_i, y_i) - h_{IC}(x_i, y_i)))^2$$

Set $\lambda_{zb,IC} = 1000$.

**Result: 9.84 mm RMSE** (wet), 4.93 mm (full domain). **5.7× improvement over v2**. Visually indistinguishable from truth except for sparse small spots in the basin (figure: `improved_v4_inversion.png`).

## Cross-experiment synthesis (thesis-relevant)

The four prior experiments established three independent equifinality breakers:
1. **Known inflow/outflow BCs** (Exp 1).
2. **Velocity observations** (Exp 1, 3).
3. **Multi-phase temporal sampling under known forcing** (Exp 2 closed basin's IC, Exp 4 tidal BC).

Exp 5 reveals a **failure mode of mechanism (3) in a closed basin with no boundary forcing**: 30 time snapshots of η+u+v are not enough to break equifinality when the only constraint is the IC and a closed boundary. The optimizer settles for fitting η perfectly with the wrong (h, z_b) split.

**A fourth equifinality breaker**: direct $z_b$ supervision derived from the IC pair $(h_{IC}, \eta_{obs}(0))$. This is structurally different from the other three — it leverages information already embedded in the IC specification rather than coming from new observations.

**Practical relevance for the Chilean tide-gauge use case**: Exp 4 already showed that 2 tidal phases of η are enough when the boundary is forced. Exp 5's negative result is a warning: in a *truly closed* domain (e.g., an isolated lake or a fjord whose mouth is far from the gauge), η-only PINN inversion will fail without auxiliary anchoring. Anchoring options: (a) known IC profile (synthetic only — implausible in field deployments), (b) sparse direct bathymetric calibration points (sonar dots), (c) tide gauge plus open-boundary forcing data.

## Limitations and next steps

- The IC z_b supervision presumes `h_IC` is known at every IC point — synthetic-friendly but not realistic for a coastal field deployment. A practical follow-up: sweep the *sparsity* of $z_b$-anchor points (5, 10, 50 known points scattered randomly in the wet disk) to find the minimum that breaks equifinality.
- The bath net's `zb_ic` residual at LBFGS end is ~1 mm RMSE on IC points but the full-disk RMSE is ~10 mm — there is a 10× residual error away from IC supervision points. A larger bath net (5 hidden × 128) or a stronger TV/Laplacian prior might close this gap.
- Causal training (Wang 2024, Fix E in the original plan) was not implemented; could be revisited if smaller error targets are needed.
- Fine-tuning the relative weights of `dry_zb`, `zb_ic`, `data`, `pde` was not exhaustive — a small grid search around the current values could yield 1–2 mm of additional improvement.
